Information about the research study

The purpose of the study is centred on collaborative learning and data analysis using learning analytics. Students taking the IS102 CAT course across multiple sections can engage in informal and stress-free discussions using a Question-and-Answer (Q&A) platform and Telegram. Each student will create his/her own Avatar identity to participate in Q&A and earn QA coins which is part of the gamification process. QA coins earned can be used as bounty to incentivise quick responses to urgent questions. The system’s text analytics ability will be able to assess students’ questions and answers and provide instant feedback to encourage students to ask better questions and provide better answers. Through the process of asking good questions, and providing good answers, we believe that students’ learning can be improved beyond the traditional classroom, and beyond instructor-student relationship. Through the platform, the students’ learning progress, knowledge acquisition, behavior and relationships can be measured and studied using text mining and social network methodologies. In addition, a chat bot will be part of the community to answer questions and solicit students’ feedback on the quality of its answers, and the platform will also direct questions to suitable students, to ensure that students remain engaged on the platform.

 

What do I need to do if I choose to take part in this study?

Participation in this Q&A platform is part of the assessment for this course, so it is compulsory.  The course instructor will allocate assessment marks based on your participation. However, participation in this Q&A platform is different than you providing consent for us to use the data captured for our research. You will need to give us your explicit consent to permit us to do so. You will not be penalized if you choose not to give us your consent (i.e. there is no affect on your grades). It is important to note that you can withdraw from your initial consent at any point in time, until such time when your course assessment grade has been submitted, should you choose to do so. The system will record every question and/or answer that you post on the platform and (with your consent) use them for research purpose, to achieve the objectives of the study stated above.   

 

What are the possible risks or discomforts?

Your Avatar identity will be used to protect your real identity during the Q&A participation, unless you specifically request that you be identified by your real name. Your participation in this study will not pose any physical or emotional risk to you. The instructors for IS102 CAT course are also the investigators of this research study, however they will not know whether you have given your consent to providing your data for research purposes. The research associate will be the one who will be collecting the informed consent forms from you and will be the only one who will have access to this information. Your identity and personal information will be removed and delinked from the Avatar identity once your final course grade has been submitted.

 

What are the possible benefits for me or others?

Your main benefit will come from learning and interacting from your peers outside the classroom and beyond your own group of friends and beyond your section, via the platform. The learning process is informal and occurs in a safe environment.   This study is designed to learn more about the learning process and learning outcome and the results obtained will be useful to help future students of the course, when the question and answer repository becomes richer and the system becomes more intelligent.

 

How will you protect the information you collect about me, and how will that information be shared?

Results of this study may be used in publications and presentations, mainly in the form of aggregated data and derived information from data related to the Avatar only.  Data related to individual students will be handled confidentially with the utmost care and security level as required by the university, including limited access to only certain individuals of the research team. Anonymized data from this study may be shared with qualified researchers or research institutions, where deemed appropriate, consistent with academic association, journal, or university policies.
